I will now explain the methodology and the brain behind our project: the Deep Learning models.

When we started, we knew that a simple model would not be good enough for complex medical images like an MRI. So, we decided to use an advanced technique called a "Dual-Branch Ensemble Model." This means we combined the power of two of the best AI models available today.

The first model is EfficientNetV2B0. This model is very fast, lightweight, and incredibly good at picking up fine details and patterns in the brain scans. 
The second model is ResNet50V2. This model uses a deep architecture that is very strong at understanding the overall structure of the image without losing important data.

By combining them side-by-side, our system looks at the MRI scan through two different lenses at the same time. The features from both models are then combined to make one final, highly accurate prediction.

Technically speaking, we extract feature maps from both networks simultaneously, concatenate their outputs into a single vector, and pass them through dense classification layers. This parallel pipeline ensures we capture both micro-levels of cellular details and the macro-structures of the brain without losing any critical medical data!

We trained this model over many epochs, carefully monitoring the loss and accuracy to make sure it was learning perfectly.

Our approach proves that two AI models working together are much better than one. Next, Jobayer will share our final results and give you a quick showcase of how it works in real life. Thank you.
